01
Products and practice

Two lanes, both visible from the front door.

Arcana should read as both a product company and a strong technical studio. This pass makes that balance more explicit without flattening either side into generic cards.

Products

Use the system directly.

The product side is for developers and teams who want runtimes, orchestration, and better command surfaces for working with agents day to day.

  • Open foundations imp and mana stay free, composable, and useful on their own.
  • Convenience on top Wizard and Familiar package the workflow into something easier to adopt and live in.
  • Trust as part of the product Review, verification, and durable work state are part of the value proposition.
Consulting

Bring Arcana in for the hard parts.

The consulting side is for teams building agent products, internal workflow systems, or developer tools that need more than a model wrapper and a slick demo.

  • Product shaping Clarify what should be automated, supervised, sold, and measured.
  • System architecture Design control surfaces, failure handling, approvals, and review paths.
  • Implementation Go from rough prototype to something that survives real users and real teams.

03
Consulting

Specific help for teams building agent products.

This section is intentionally less generic and a bit more architectural. It should feel like real work, not a vague services menu.

01 — Product shaping

Find the real operator and the real wedge.

Clarify which part of the workflow deserves to become a product, where supervision is required, and what needs to be visible for people to trust the system.

02 — System design

Build the machinery around the model.

Review flows, approvals, failure handling, permissions, budgets, and operator tools matter as much as generation itself.

03 — Delivery

Turn the prototype into something people can live with.

Sharpen the UX, strengthen the architecture, and harden the workflow until it can survive real users instead of collapsing after the first polished demo.
